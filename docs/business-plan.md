# Hello Lang Business Plan

Last updated: 2026-04-24

## 1. Executive summary

Hello Lang is a mobile-first AI language learning product for Japanese users who often know what they want to say but cannot instantly convert everyday thoughts into natural English.

Instead of starting from grammar lessons, Hello Lang starts from urgency and self-expression. A user speaks or types what they want to say in Japanese, gets a natural English phrase immediately, saves it automatically, and then turns that phrase into repeatable study material.

The business opportunity is to own the layer between translation utility and memory formation. Existing products usually do one of these well but not both:

- translators solve the immediate output problem
- textbook apps solve structured study
- flashcard apps solve storage

Hello Lang combines all three in one emotional moment: "I want to say this now."

## 2. Problem

Target users face three recurring pains:

- they can understand some English but cannot produce natural spoken phrases on demand
- generic translators often sound stiff, literal, or emotionally wrong
- phrases they looked up once are forgotten because there is no built-in memorization loop

For Japanese learners, this is especially painful in:

- travel
- casual conversation
- work communication
- dating and social messaging
- emotionally charged situations where nuance matters

## 3. Solution

Hello Lang provides an AI-assisted phrase capture and memorization loop:

1. User inputs a Japanese phrase by text or voice.
2. AI returns natural English plus a category.
3. The phrase is saved instantly with zero extra effort.
4. The app reads it aloud.
5. The user reviews it later via cards and a searchable phrase notebook.

This creates a strong product promise:

"Say what you actually mean in English, then make it stick."

## 4. Product positioning

### Category

AI-powered phrasebook and active memorization app.

### Positioning statement

For Japanese speakers who want natural spoken English quickly, Hello Lang is the fastest way to turn real-life thoughts into usable English and remember them later, unlike translators that forget context and flashcard apps that require manual setup.

### Why now

- AI now makes natural phrase generation cheap and fast enough for consumer use
- mobile speech input lowers friction for in-the-moment capture
- users are more willing to study through utility than through curriculum-heavy apps

## 5. Target customers

### Primary segment

Japanese adults aged roughly 18 to 39 who:

- travel internationally
- consume English media
- want better spoken confidence
- prefer lightweight mobile tools over formal classes

### Secondary segments

- job seekers preparing for interviews or international communication
- hospitality and retail workers needing practical phrase recall
- anime, music, and pop-culture fans who want social English output
- students preparing for exchange or study abroad

## 6. Core value proposition

Hello Lang wins if it feels:

- faster than opening a textbook
- more natural than literal translation
- easier than creating flashcards manually
- more personal than lesson-based apps

The core emotional benefit is confidence.

The core functional benefit is reusable, personalized phrase memory.

## 7. Business model

### Recommended model

Freemium subscription.

### Free tier

- limited daily translations
- local phrase storage
- basic flashcard review
- email signup optional

### Paid tier

- unlimited translations
- cloud sync across devices
- pronunciation practice
- phrase collections and folders
- advanced review system
- travel/work themed packs
- history insights and weekly progress reports

### Indicative pricing

- monthly: 680 to 980 JPY
- annual: 6,800 to 9,800 JPY

This price range is low enough for impulse utility and high enough for a focused single-purpose app with recurring value.

## 8. Go-to-market strategy

### Phase 1: founder-led organic acquisition

- short-form social content showing "how do I say this in English?" moments
- before/after phrase transformation examples
- creator content around travel, dating, work, and awkward real-life situations
- founder-led product building updates tied to user feedback

### Phase 2: retention and referrals

- weekly phrase recap emails
- shareable phrase cards
- streaks and collections users want to keep
- invite-based premium trials

### Phase 3: partnerships

- micro-influencers in travel and English learning
- Japanese study-abroad communities
- language schools or coaching partners

## 9. Competitive advantage

Hello Lang can differentiate on five dimensions:

- context-rich phrase generation instead of literal translation
- zero-friction saving of phrases during the translation moment
- built-in study flow from day one
- voice capture for mobile, emotional, real-life moments
- warm and lifestyle-oriented brand rather than school-like edtech branding

## 10. Current traction assumptions

The repository suggests the company is in pre-seed or solo-founder validation stage:

- product is already deployed
- auth and cloud persistence exist
- metrics tracking mindset exists through reporting scripts
- monetization and analytics are not yet implemented

The most realistic near-term business goal is not revenue maximization, but proving repeat usage and phrase accumulation.

## 11. Key metrics

### North star metric

Saved phrases per weekly active user.

This measures whether Hello Lang is solving both utility and retention.

### Supporting metrics

- daily active users
- weekly active users
- new user activation rate
- translations per active user
- save rate per translation
- percentage of users who review cards within 7 days
- 7-day retention
- 30-day retention
- percentage of phrases marked mastered
- paid conversion rate

## 12. Financial view

### Current cost structure

Likely low at current scale:

- Vercel
- Supabase
- Gemini usage
- domain
- optional email and notification vendors

### Financial thesis

If the app can achieve strong repeat behavior, contribution margin should be attractive because:

- phrase generation requests are short
- review sessions are cheap once phrases are saved
- study value compounds over time as personal history grows

## 13. Risks

### Product risk

Users may treat the app as a one-off translator rather than a study habit.

### Retention risk

Without reminders, goals, or spaced repetition, saved phrases may not turn into repeat sessions.

### Differentiation risk

Generic AI chat tools can also translate phrases, so Hello Lang must win on speed, organization, and memory outcomes.

### Trust risk

Users may not trust translations in sensitive or emotional contexts unless examples and nuance handling improve.

## 14. Strategic priorities for the next 6 months

- improve activation from first translation to first saved phrase to first review session
- add habit loops that increase weekly return rate
- build analytics instrumentation before scaling acquisition
- launch a clear premium reason to pay
- narrow positioning around a few high-intent use cases

## 15. Recommended business thesis

Hello Lang should not try to become a broad language school app.

It should become the default personal phrase memory tool for Japanese users who want to express themselves naturally in English. The strongest moat is not raw model output. The strongest moat is the user's accumulated phrase history, review habit, and trust in the app's tone.
